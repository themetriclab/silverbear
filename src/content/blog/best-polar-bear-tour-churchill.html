The Peak Season: October and November Polar Bear Photography in Churchill

Why These Months Are Prime

Polar bears fast on land during summer and wait for sea ice to return. The freshwater-fed Hudson Bay freezes early near Churchill, attracting bears back beginning in mid-October through mid-November.

This timing means:

  • The highest concentration of bears gathered along the coast.
  • A dynamic season with changing tundra scenery from autumn colours to early snow.
  • Sharp and soft fall light ideal for photography.

What to Expect in the Field

During your October-November tour, you'll likely see:

  • Single bears, playful young males sparring, and mother bears with cubs.
  • Bears moving along beaches or lounging on tundra and snow.
  • Increasing ice and snow that add dramatic foregrounds and reflections.

Weather and Lighting

  • Temperatures range from just below freezing to mild cold — perfect for fieldwork with proper clothing.
  • Soft, overcast light reduces harsh shadows on white fur and snow.
  • Long twilight hours provide beautiful golden and blue light portraits.

Q: How cold is it?
